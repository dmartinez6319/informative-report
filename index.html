AI's Ethical Challenges in Society

Artificial Intellgience is becoming more mainstream and used in everyday activities, but that comes at a cost.

By Daniel Martinez March 2025

Artificial Intelligence in the Modern World

Artificial intelligence is becoming increasingly more popular and common use in the modern world since it is very accessible through a website and as a result is starting to impact and change the way we interact with the world. Artificial Intelligence has its many benefits such as increasing productivity, solving complex problems, organizing data, but all of this comes at a cost. (Bombalier) It has many issues or problems such as handling very private data and the many ethical or moral issues that it is being used for. All of these factors in artificial intelligence have made many people develop mixed feelings on what role artificial intelligence should have in this world. In this report I will go over the many key issues around artificial intelligence and how it is impacting society in particular education institutions along with presenting how artificial intelligence is seen by the school’s perspectives. how there are possible solutions and measures taken to use artificial intelligence to our advantage without the ethical or moral issues it presents.

Privacy and Data Concerns

Artificial intelligence systems usually rely on a large amount of personal data to function effectively through answering any personalized question. This is what allows artificial intelligence to have personalization and almost instant services but at the same time it also introduces very big privacy risks. The collection, storage, and use of this private data raise concerns about unauthorized access, data breaches, and surveillance. In many cases, the data collected by artificial intelligence systems can be used in ways that individuals might not fully understand or agree to which poses a very big ethical and moral problem by the companies that create it. Through this source, The political philosophy of AI, Coeckelbergh (2022) who is an expert in this field of technology and is highly regarded says that "the extensive data collection by AI systems poses threats to individual privacy and autonomy". This shows that as artificial intelligence becomes more prevalent in many sectors of life keeping personal information safe is becoming more crucial than ever.

Bias and Unfair of Artificial Intelligence responses

Something that is often not considered when receiving artificial intelligence is how the responses are biased depending on various factors. Most artificial intelligence developed is reliant on the information that its creators train it with and depending on what type of information it is given will determine how its interactions are made. This type of bias in artificial intelligence is called machine learning bias or algorithm bias according to IBM (Holdsworth, 2025) and could lead to potentially very dangerous outcomes. For example, old biased information that artificial intelligence analyzes could be used in an interaction which fosters incorrect, outdated, or unfair judgements especially with historically marginalized groups. This shows that artificial intelligence may lack perspectives or morals when making decisions which is something important to consider when regulating artificial intelligence.

Misuse of Artificial Intelligence

Another issue surrounding artificial intelligence is its potential misuse. While artificial intelligence has the ability to increase productivity in many areas of life, it can also be used for harmful or cheating purposes. The risk of artificial intelligence being used to manipulate public opinion will be a big problem especially on social media to spread lies and track people. Another very big area of concern is it being used to gain unfair advantage over others especially in schools due to most artificial intelligence being capable of answering any question. This is shown through a Vatican document as it reports, “merely provide answers instead of prompting students to arrive at answers themselves or write text for themselves”; which can lead to a failure to develop critical thinking skills.” (Cernuzio, 2025) The innovations of artificial intelligence have brought many use cases or benefits, however in the wrong hands for the wrong purpose can be very consequential.

Perspectives on Artificial Intelligence

The impact of AI on education has shown varying perspectives among educators or instructors. Many teachers recognize the potential benefits of artificial intelligence as it enhances personalized learning and increases student engagement with school lessons and curriculum. According to a report by Imagine Learning (2024) , 55% of surveyed educators view generative artificial intelligence as having a positive impact in the classroom, while 88% believe artificial intelligence can improve accessibility for students that may need extra personalization for their work. However, there are still concerns shown with ethical or moral problems of using artificial intelligence along with data privacy of educators and students. In another survey done by EDUCAUSE, it was reported, “respondents also believe that academic dishonesty will increase (64%) and that students will trust AI tools too much (60%).” (Robert, 2024) The role of artificial intelligence in educational institutions is uncertain but becomes clearer as shown next in a case study under regulation.

Case Study on Artificial Intelligence

The use of artificial intelligence in educational settings can offer significant benefits despite its possible ethical misuse if it is regulated and balanced right as shown from this case study, The AI Assessment Scale (AIAS) in action: A pilot implementation of genai-supported assessment (2024) . This case study went over how artificial intelligence supported assessments can help multiple educational institutions by providing "more efficient and personalized learning experiences" (2024) . Through integrating artificial intelligence in the school and monitoring how students interacted with it, the school was able to significantly increase student scores while preventing moral and ethical issues of misuse. This has shown that artificial intelligence can be of some benefit to society as shown from this study in a school setting if action is done to make sure regulations are set in place to prevent moral or ethical issues that it comes with.

Regulation of Artificial Intelligence

As artificial intelligence technology advances, governments worldwide have started implementing policies to address ethical, safety, and transparency concerns. The European Union has taken a significant step by laying down the AI Act, which regulates artificial intelligence by ensuring artificial intelligence are systems designed to be safe, fair, and managed with human oversight (EU AI Act, 2025).

Conclusion

In conclusion, while artificial intelligence can offer a great deal of benefits, such as increased productivity and personalized learning, its ethical or moral problems must be considered and not overlooked. The different views or perspectives on artificial intelligence in education show the need for balanced regulation to make sure it’s responsibly used. By paying attention to these problems, artificial intelligence can be used to potentially improve society without its moral and ethical problems. As technology continues to improve, it is important to be attentive to the changes that shape our world such as artificial intelligence.
